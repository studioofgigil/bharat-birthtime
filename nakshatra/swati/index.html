Swati Nakshatra

The independent — wind-blown freedom, balance and self-reliance.

At a glance

Ruling planet (dasha lord)Rahu
Presiding deityVayu (the wind)
SymbolCoral, sword, or a young shoot bending in the wind
Gana (temperament)Deva
Naming syllables (pada 1–4)Ru, Re, Ro, Ta
Position in zodiacNakshatra 15 of 27

Meaning & mythology

Swati is governed by Vayu, the wind, and symbolised by a young plant bending yet unbroken in the breeze. It is the nakshatra of independence, movement and self-determination. Tradition associates Swati with flexibility, diplomacy, a love of freedom, fairness in dealings, adaptability and the quiet strength to stand alone and find one's own balance.

Naming syllables (Namakaran)

If a child is born under Swati nakshatra, tradition suggests a name beginning with one of these syllables, chosen by the Moon's pada (quarter) at birth: Ru, Re, Ro, Ta — one syllable for each of the four padas.

These are the classical syllables; regional traditions vary slightly. For ritual decisions, confirm with your family panchanga or purohit.
