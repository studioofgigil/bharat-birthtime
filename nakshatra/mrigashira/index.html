Mrigashira Nakshatra

The searching deer — curiosity, gentleness and the seeking mind.

At a glance

Ruling planet (dasha lord)Mars
Presiding deitySoma (the Moon / nectar of immortality)
SymbolDeer's head
Gana (temperament)Deva
Naming syllables (pada 1–4)Ve, Vo, Ka, Ki
Position in zodiacNakshatra 5 of 27

Meaning & mythology

Mrigashira means 'the deer's head'. Ruled by Soma, the lunar nectar, it carries the restless, gentle energy of a deer in search — forever seeking the sweet and the beautiful. Tradition associates this nakshatra with curiosity, a roving and inquisitive mind, sensitivity, a love of exploration and an eternal quest for something just beyond reach.

Naming syllables (Namakaran)

If a child is born under Mrigashira nakshatra, tradition suggests a name beginning with one of these syllables, chosen by the Moon's pada (quarter) at birth: Ve, Vo, Ka, Ki — one syllable for each of the four padas.

These are the classical syllables; regional traditions vary slightly. For ritual decisions, confirm with your family panchanga or purohit.
